Zebras

Zebras are the cousins of horses and are native to Africa. They have black and white stripes that come in different patterns that're unique to the individual zebra.

Diet

Zebras are grazers and eat mostly grass. Sometimes they'll eat bark, leaves and fruits. Depending on the amount of food available to them, they can spend 60-80% of their lives eating. They are more water dependent than other species so it's easy to find them near water sources.

Conservation

Grevy's zebras are endangered, mountain zebras are vulnerable and plains zebras are near threatened. All zebras are threatened by hunting and habitat destruction. Luckily, there are a range of protection areas in countries such as Kenya, Ethiopia, Tanzania, Namibia, and South Africa.
